Engineering Director – Emergency Management Role

Activation Criteria

This role is activated as part of the Full Emergency Management Team (EMT) mobilization, as described in ERP Section 2.5.2. The Engineering Director provides direct technical and operational support during any aviation-related emergency involving aircraft status, maintenance, or engineering considerations.

Core Responsibilities

ERP Section 3.6.4 outlines the following duties:

  • Act as the primary technical authority on all engineering matters during an emergency.
  • Coordinate with the CAMO, Maintenance Manager, and Safety Director to retrieve, secure, and protect technical records.
  • Assist in the preservation of aircraft wreckage or key components in compliance with GCAA and ERP protocols.
  • Support the investigation team by providing aircraft maintenance status, open MEL items, and technical records upon request.
  • Ensure engineering resources are allocated to support recovery or investigation efforts, including site visits or forensic documentation.

Checklist Reference

Appendix C.8 – Engineering Director Checklist must be activated and followed. This includes:

  • Immediate coordination with OCC and CAMO upon activation
  • Retrieval and digital backup of technical logs, work packages, and AD/SB compliance records
  • Securing of maintenance data (FDR, CVR if applicable) and handing over to Safety Director or GCAA as needed

Response Phases

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Confirm technical readiness of aircraft, review last maintenance action, support Safety/OCC with any tech data required.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Join strategy meetings with EMT, assist with technical narrative and incident background, retrieve maintenance releases or deferred defects.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Draft and review technical sections of the GCAA preliminary report, coordinate with OEM if engineering input is required for fault isolation or failure analysis.

Restrictions & Compliance

  • Do not disclose technical findings outside the EMT or GCAA unless explicitly approved by the Accountable Manager.
  • Ensure all digital and physical records are secured and archived to SMS-Pro for audit trail integrity.
  • Preserve aircraft systems and parts involved in the incident until officially released by the investigative body.
