SIMULATION SCENARIO: AIRCRAFT ACCIDENT

Training / awareness scenario (for learning context)
TRAINING SIMULATION – NOT FOR OPERATIONAL USE This page presents an exercise timeline and ERP references to support learning and awareness. It does not provide operational instructions or decision-making guidance.

Scenario Summary

Scenario TitleOffshore Crash – Bell 412 Helicopter
Date/TimeTuesday, 24 June 2025
FlightADA412 — Zayed International Airport to Umm Lulu Platform
Onboard2 crew, 6 passengers
Show / Hide Simulation Timeline
  • 03:45 – LOSS OF CONTACT (Confirmed by OCC Duty Coordinator)
    • The Operations Control Centre (OCC) attempts to establish contact with the aircraft using VHF, HF, and ACARS, but no response is received • The incident is formally recorded by opening the Incident Log using Form B.7 to ensure traceability from the first confirmed trigger • The Duty Director of Operations (DOO) is notified to ensure immediate leadership awareness and enable escalation if required • Aircraft identity, route, and last known position are recorded in the OCC log to build the initial situational picture for coordination and follow-up
    References:
    ERP 2.6.2 Checklist: Appendix B.3 Form: Appendix B.7
  • 03:48 – ELT SIGNAL DETECTED
    • An Emergency Locator Transmitter (ELT) signal is detected via satellite and relayed to OCC by ATC, indicating a probable accident scenario • Available data is used to triangulate an approximate position to establish an initial focus area for response coordination • The DOO initiates the Quick Response Emergency Management Team to begin structured internal coordination and rapid situational assessment • The Accountable Manager is contacted to ensure senior leadership awareness and to support escalation to full EMT mobilisation if required
    References:
    ERP 2.5.1 ERP 2.6.2
  • 04:00 – EMT MOBILISED AND CONVENED IN EMC
    • The Emergency Management Team (EMT) is convened in the Emergency Management Centre (EMC) to coordinate the organisational response • Core functional representatives assemble to ensure operational control, safety oversight, people-support, communications, and coordination are covered • The initial meeting establishes shared situational awareness and confirms early priorities while formal emergency roles begin operating under the ERP structure
    References:
    ERP 3.3 ERP 3.6 Checklists Activated: C.1 C.3 C.4 C.5 C.6
  • 04:05 – FIELD SITE TEAM (FST) DEPLOYED
    • A response helicopter is dispatched from AUH to establish on-scene awareness and support coordination with offshore responders • The deployed team includes a Search & Rescue specialist, an FST representative, and emergency supplies to support early response activity • ADNOC Offshore is notified and confirms vessel dispatch to assist with offshore access, coordination, and emergency support
    References:
    ERP 3.4.5 Checklist: Appendix C.27 Appendix C.28 Appendix C.26
  • 04:15 – FAMILY ASSISTANCE ACTIVATED
    • The Family Assistance Centre (FAC) is established at ADA HQ to provide a controlled, private environment for next-of-kin support • Next-of-kin contact begins via the HR Manager to ensure notifications are coordinated and managed through a single controlled channel • Go-Crisis is activated as a specialist crisis-support provider in line with the existing agreement referenced in the ERP appendices • Immediate welfare support arrangements are put in place (quiet rooms, refreshments, and psychological support) to stabilise the family-support environment
    References:
    ERP Chapter 5 Appendix D.1–D.3 Appendix A.1
  • 04:30 – MEDIA COMMUNICATION CONTROLLED
    • A controlled holding statement is used to acknowledge the incident while avoiding speculation and protecting the accuracy of public information • External communications control is reinforced so that only authorised personnel release information in line with ERP communication requirements • The Communications Officer monitors media coverage and prepares structured responses to reduce misinformation risk and protect organisational integrity
    References:
    ERP 1.15
  • 05:10 – SITE CONFIRMED BY FST
    • The Field Site Team confirms the accident site location and provides an initial description of the scene to the EMC • ADNOC emergency services confirm presence on-site and begin coordinated rescue and extraction activity • Casualty status is reported in categories to support medical response coordination and internal situational reporting • The FST begins structured situation reporting back to the EMC to support ongoing coordination and leadership decision support
    References:
    ERP 3.5.1 ERP 3.4.5
  • 05:25 – COORDINATION WITH NATIONAL AGENCIES
    • Notifications to national agencies are initiated through the Safety Director to meet regulatory and emergency coordination expectations • Key stakeholders are contacted (including aviation and national emergency entities) to ensure aligned response coordination and reporting • Aircraft Accident Notification documentation is completed using Form B.5 to support formal reporting and traceable records
    References:
    ERP 2.3 Form: Appendix B.5
  • 06:00 – EMC STRATEGY SESSION
    • The EMC conducts a structured strategy session led by the DOO, informed by updates from the OCC Manager and the Field Site Team • The session aligns the team on the current situation picture, key priorities, and the transition from immediate response to managed recovery planning • Recovery coordination, evidence preservation awareness, insurance notification, and legal coordination needs are reviewed at a familiarisation level • Go-Crisis and legal support are aligned to prepare for controlled passenger and stakeholder communications as the situation develops
    References:
    ERP 3.5.1 ERP 3.8.3 ERP 3.8.6
  • 07:30 – ADA EXECUTIVE BRIEFING (Chair: CEO)
    • An executive-level briefing is convened to ensure leadership oversight, strategic alignment, and clear internal governance during the response • Key leaders review the emerging situation picture and confirm internal coordination across operational, safety, and support functions • Preliminary regulatory reporting is discussed at an awareness level to ensure readiness for required submissions and external engagement • Response records and supporting documentation are reinforced as controlled evidence and backed up in the SMS system to support audit and investigation needs
    References:
    ERP 0.1 ERP 1.4.5 ERP 3.7
  • 10:00 – POST-RESPONSE: INITIAL REPORTING & CLEANUP
    • Occupant status is confirmed based on rescue and medical information as the situation stabilises • Survivors are evacuated to hospital care, and fatalities are formally accounted for through controlled reporting channels • OCC functions progressively return toward normal operations as emergency coordination reduces and the recovery phase begins • The EMT transitions from immediate response into recovery and investigation support activities, ensuring records remain traceable and controlled
    References:
    ERP 3.8.2 ERP 3.8.3
  • Day +2 – FOLLOW-UP ACTIONS
    • A structured debrief is conducted with EMT members to capture observations, lessons, and improvement opportunities from the response • All incident documentation is uploaded to the SMS system to ensure completeness, retention, and easy retrieval for audit or investigation • The response timeline is reviewed as a learning tool to strengthen organisational readiness and familiarisation with ERP coordination flow • An ERP review is scheduled and recommendations are logged to support continuous improvement and governance follow-up
    References:
    ERP 0.1 ERP 0.3.1 ERP 1.13
