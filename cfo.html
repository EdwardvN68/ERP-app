Chief Financial Officer (Emergency Management Team Member)

Role Context (Training Overview)

As Chief Financial Officer (CFO), you provide financial authority, oversight, and assurance to the Emergency Management Team (EMT). Your role supports rapid response resourcing while maintaining traceability and accountability.

Activation (ERP-Aligned Awareness)

The CFO is engaged during emergencies where there is potential financial, insurance, or compensatory exposure, as directed by EMT leadership.

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.13 – Chief Financial Officer.

Core Responsibilities (Explained for Training)

  • Support EMT financial needs through appropriate budget, accounting, and purchasing provisions when required.
  • Support rapid procurement of necessary equipment as required by company departments during emergency response.

ERP References

  • Section 3.6.13 – Chief Financial Officer
  • Appendix C.13 – CFO Checklist

Role Behaviour Across an Emergency (Training Interpretation)

  • Early phase: Ensure financial support pathways are ready and responsive.
  • Sustained response: Maintain financial traceability and support urgent procurement needs.
  • Stabilisation / recovery: Support reconciliation and documentation for review and audit readiness.

Cautions

  • Maintain traceability for emergency expenditure and purchasing decisions.
  • Support rapid procurement when required, while ensuring appropriate oversight.
