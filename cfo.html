Chief Financial Officer – Emergency Response Role

Activation

The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) is activated during Medium and High-level emergencies that may result in significant financial, insurance, or compensatory exposure. Activation is triggered by the EMT Chairperson or Accountable Manager as part of the core Emergency Management Team (EMT) response.

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.11 – Chief Financial Officer.

Core Responsibilities

  • Act as the EMT’s focal point for all financial and insurance matters arising from the emergency.
  • Establish and manage the budget for emergency-related costs, including travel, accommodation, family assistance, and external contractors (e.g., Go Crisis).
  • Provide immediate financial authority for time-sensitive expenditures in support of the EMT.
  • Coordinate closely with the Legal Advisor and Insurance Broker to ensure compliance with coverage terms and notify underwriters as required.
  • Arrange emergency cash disbursement procedures and credit authorizations.
  • Monitor and report on the overall cost impact of the emergency to the EMT Chairperson and Accountable Manager.
  • Liaise with ADA’s finance and procurement departments to expedite required goods and services.

ERP References

  • Section 3.6.11 – Chief Financial Officer

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Confirm EMT activation status and readiness. Notify insurance broker (if applicable). Activate rapid expense approval protocol. Coordinate with Go Crisis or emergency vendors if deployed.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Establish tracking for all emergency expenses. Issue financial delegation instructions to EMT support roles. Secure travel and accommodation funding for NOK and response teams.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Compile financial impact assessments. Support post-event reporting for Board and regulators. Maintain financial transparency records for audit.

Cautions

  • Do not delay financial approvals for response-critical tasks (e.g., aircraft dispatch, family assistance).
  • Avoid exceeding coverage limits—coordinate with legal and insurers first.
  • Do not delegate expenditure authority without written or logged approval.
  • Ensure documentation of all costs for post-incident reconciliation.
