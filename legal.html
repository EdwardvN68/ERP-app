Chief Legal Advisor – Emergency Response Role

Activation

The Chief Legal Advisor is activated during Medium and High-level emergencies, particularly those involving fatalities, legal liability, data preservation, or insurer coordination. Activation is typically directed by the EMT Chairperson or Accountable Manager as part of the Emergency Management Team (EMT).

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.13 – Chief Legal Advisor.

Core Responsibilities

  • Provide immediate legal guidance to the EMT, ensuring all emergency actions comply with applicable laws and contractual obligations.
  • Advise the Accountable Manager and EMT Chairperson on exposure to criminal or civil liability in relation to the incident.
  • Coordinate with the Insurance Broker and CFO regarding liability coverage, claims notification, and insurer engagement.
  • Oversee the collection and legal safeguarding of all documentary evidence including voice recordings, flight documentation, personnel records, and maintenance data.
  • Guide internal teams on their legal obligations regarding privacy, data sharing, and regulatory reporting.
  • Act as the primary liaison with government agencies, civil aviation authorities, and law enforcement regarding legal matters.
  • Assist in drafting and approving all public statements or media releases in coordination with the EMT Spokesperson and Commercial Director.

ERP References

  • Section 3.6.13 – Chief Legal Advisor

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Alerted and briefed by EMT Chair or Accountable Manager. Identify immediate legal risks and advise EMT accordingly. Establish secure documentation channels and initiate evidence protection.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Coordinate with insurers and CFO for legal alignment of claims. Review potential external communications for legal exposure. Engage with civil aviation authority if notification obligations arise.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Continue advisory role as situation evolves (investigation, litigation, etc.). Ensure secure handover of evidence to investigating authorities. Maintain legal log of decisions, actions, and communications.

Cautions

  • Do not allow any destruction, deletion, or editing of potential evidence.
  • Do not issue public statements without legal vetting.
  • Do not disclose information to third parties unless legally authorized.
  • Ensure all document handling complies with data protection and confidentiality laws.
  • Avoid speculative statements in internal or external settings.
