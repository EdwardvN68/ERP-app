SIMULATION SCENARIO: INFLIGHT FIRE

Training / awareness scenario (for learning context)
TRAINING SIMULATION – NOT FOR OPERATIONAL USE This page presents an exercise timeline and ERP references to support learning and awareness. It does not provide operational instructions or decision-making guidance.

Scenario Summary

Scenario Title Smoke in Cabin – ADA Dash 8 Q400 en route to Sir Bani Yas
Date Friday, 27 June 2025
Flight ADA232 — Zayed International Airport to Sir Bani Yas Island
Aircraft / Reg Dash 8 Q400 — A6-ADK
Crew / Pax 2 pilots, 1 cabin crew / 17 passengers
Weather Daylight VMC, calm winds
Show / Hide Simulation Timeline
  • 08:20 – SMOKE DETECTED IN CABIN MID-FLIGHT
    • While climbing approximately 20 NM northwest of Zayed International Airport, smoke is reported by cabin crew from an overhead panel near row 6 • The flight crew declare PAN-PAN to Air Traffic Control, escalating to MAYDAY as the seriousness of the situation becomes clear • A decision is made to return immediately to AUH as a precautionary emergency measure • Fire extinguishers are deployed within the cabin, with no visible flames observed
    References:
    ERP 2.6.1.3 ERP 2.6.2.5
  • 08:22 – OCC NOTIFIED
    • Air Traffic Control informs the OCC Duty Coordinator that flight ADA232 has declared an emergency due to smoke in the cabin • The OCC formally opens an Incident Log to ensure traceability from the first notification • The Duty Director of Operations and Safety Director are informed immediately to ensure senior oversight • OCC coordination activities begin using the appropriate checklist to support structured response
    References:
    ERP 2.3 ERP 2.6.2.5 Checklist: Appendix B.3 Form: Appendix B.6
  • 08:24 – AIRPORT EMERGENCY SERVICES MOBILISED
    • Airport emergency response services are activated by ATC in anticipation of an emergency landing • Fire and Rescue services at AUH are placed on standby, along with ambulance and crash response teams • The OCC alerts the EMT Chair to prepare for possible Quick Response EMT activation
    References:
    ERP 2.8 ERP 2.5.1
  • 08:31 – SAFE LANDING AT AUH
    • The Dash 8 aircraft lands safely at Zayed International Airport with priority clearance • A precautionary evacuation is conducted following landing to ensure passenger and crew safety • Two passengers report symptoms consistent with smoke inhalation, with no serious injuries identified • The aircraft is moved to an appropriate stand to allow inspection and further assessment
    References:
    ERP 2.8 ERP 2.15
  • 08:45 – EMT QUICK RESPONSE MOBILISED
    • The Duty Director of Operations confirms activation of the Quick Response Emergency Management Team • EMT members convene in the Emergency Management Centre to coordinate post-landing response and oversight
    References:
    ERP 2.5.1 ERP 3.3 ERP 3.6
  • 09:00 – COMMUNICATIONS ACTIONED
    • An internal staff bulletin is issued to confirm a technical emergency with no fatalities reported • A holding statement is prepared and released as required, maintaining control over external communications • Passenger identities are not disclosed in accordance with communication and privacy requirements
    References:
    ERP 1.16.3 ERP 1.16.5 ERP 1.16.7
  • 09:30 – TECHNICAL AND SAFETY INVESTIGATION BEGINS
    • Engineering and maintenance activities are initiated to assess the technical cause of the smoke event • Safety reporting and coordination actions commence to support investigation and regulatory follow-up • Flight Data Recorder and Cockpit Voice Recorder are secured as applicable to preserve evidence
    References:
    ERP 3.7 ERP 3.8.6
  • 10:45 – PASSENGER SUPPORT & FOLLOW-UP
    • Passenger welfare follow-up activities are conducted to ensure ongoing support after the incident • Medical checks are completed for passengers affected by smoke exposure • Family Assistance arrangements are placed on standby or activated as appropriate
    References:
    ERP 2.15 Appendix D.1 Appendix D.3
  • 14:00 – EMT CLOSES INCIDENT WITH DEBRIEF
    • A structured Emergency Management Team debrief is conducted to review the response and coordination • Actions and observations are formally recorded and logged within the SMS system • An ERP review is flagged as required to support continuous improvement and readiness assurance
    References:
    ERP 1.13 ERP 0.1
