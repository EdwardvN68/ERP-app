SIMULATION SCENARIO: INFLIGHT FIRE

Title: Smoke in Cabin – ADA Dash 8 Q400 en route to Sir Bani Yas

Date: Friday, 27 June 2025

Flight: ADA232 – Zayed International Airport to Sir Bani Yas Island

Aircraft Type: Dash 8 Q400

Registration: A6-ADK

Crew: 2 pilots, 1 cabin crew

Passengers: 17

Weather: Daylight VMC, calm winds

08:20 – SMOKE DETECTED IN CABIN MID-FLIGHT
  • Location: 20 NM northwest of Zayed International Airport, climbing through 7,000 feet
  • Cabin crew reports smoke coming from overhead panel near row 6
  • Captain declares PAN-PAN to ATC, then escalates to MAYDAY
  • Decision: Immediate return to AUH
  • Fire extinguishers deployed, no visible flames
ERP Reference: ERP 2.6.3, 2.13.2, 2.13.3
08:22 – OCC NOTIFIED
  • ATC calls OCC Duty Coordinator: “ADA232 declared emergency – smoke in cabin”
  • OCC initiates Incident Log (Form B.6)
  • DOO and Safety Director informed immediately
  • Duty Operations Coordinator begins checklist (Appendix B.3)
ERP Reference: ERP 2.3, 2.13.2, 2.13.4; Forms: B.6, B.5
08:24 – AIRPORT EMERGENCY SERVICES MOBILISED
  • ATC activates full airport emergency response
  • AUH Fire & Rescue on standby on Runway 31L
  • Ambulances and Crash Response Teams deployed
  • OCC alerts EMT Chair for possible Quick Response activation
ERP Reference: ERP 2.13.1, 2.8, 3.6.2
08:31 – SAFE LANDING AT AUH
  • Dash 8 lands safely with priority clearance
  • Immediate evacuation via main entry door
  • 2 passengers report smoke inhalation; no serious injuries
  • Aircraft towed to isolated stand for inspection
ERP Reference: ERP 2.13.4, 4.7
08:45 – EMT QUICK RESPONSE MOBILISED
  • Confirmed by DOO
  • Team Members:
    • DOO (Chair)
    • OCC Manager
    • Safety Director
    • HR Manager
    • EMC Manager
    • Communications Officer
    • Maintenance Rep
ERP Reference: ERP 3.3.1–3.3.3, 3.6.2, 3.6.4; Checklists: C.4, C.6, C.10
09:00 – COMMUNICATIONS ACTIONED
  • Internal staff bulletin sent: technical emergency, no fatalities
  • Holding statement issued:
“ADA flight ADA232 returned to AUH following the crew’s detection of smoke in the cabin. The flight landed safely and all passengers were evacuated as a precaution. Two passengers received precautionary medical checks.”
  • No media release of passenger identities
ERP Reference: ERP 1.15.3–1.15.5
09:30 – TECHNICAL AND SAFETY INVESTIGATION BEGINS
  • Maintenance Team initiates inspection with GCAA rep present
  • Suspicion: short circuit in passenger reading light wiring
  • Safety Director files preliminary report with GCAA and ADA QA
  • Flight Data Recorder and CVR secured
ERP Reference: ERP 3.7, 3.8.6
10:45 – PASSENGER SUPPORT & FOLLOW-UP
  • HR and FAC team conduct wellness calls to all 17 passengers
  • Medical reports cleared all passengers post-observation
  • ADA provides formal apology and travel voucher
  • CRM team debriefs cabin crew for stress support
ERP Reference: ERP 4.3.7, 4.13
14:00 – EMT CLOSES INCIDENT WITH DEBRIEF
  • Full root cause analysis pending
  • Immediate action:
    • Remove same lighting units across fleet pending check
    • Additional CRM training ordered for all cabin crew
    • SMS Pro record updated
    • ERP Manual flagged for review (Section 1.13)
