Safety Director (Emergency Management Team Member)

Role Context (Training Overview)

As Safety Director, you provide specialist safety and regulatory oversight to the Emergency Management Team (EMT). Your role ensures that emergency response actions remain aligned with aviation safety principles, regulatory expectations, and organisational accountability.

Activation (ERP-Aligned Awareness)

The Safety Director is engaged during medium- and high-level emergencies when the EMT is activated. Where the Accountable Manager is unavailable, the ERP defines that the Safety Director assumes the role of EMT Chairman, ensuring continuity of leadership.

Training note: Activation thresholds and leadership succession are defined in the ERP and are not discretionary.

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.2 and Appendix C.6.

Core Responsibilities (ERP 3.6.2 – Explained for Training)

Your responsibilities focus on safety assurance, regulatory liaison, and specialist advice. You support EMT decision-making by ensuring safety risks, legal obligations, and regulatory expectations are clearly understood.

  • Advise the EMT on all safety-related and emergency management matters.
  • Liaise with regulatory authorities, law enforcement, and security agencies as required.
  • Coordinate specialist assistance when necessary (e.g. safety investigation support, subject-matter experts).
  • Ensure that engagement with authorities is properly established and coordinated.
  • Keep the EMT informed of regulatory and authority expectations during the response.
  • Act as the authorising authority for activation of the Crisis Management Service Provider (Go Crisis), when required.

Role Behaviour Across an Emergency (Training Interpretation)

Training note: This section supports understanding of role behaviour and does not replace ERP checklists.

  • Early phase: Confirm safety-critical information, establish regulatory liaison, and support EMT situational awareness.
  • Sustained response: Advise on safety risks, regulatory obligations, and investigation considerations.
  • Stabilisation / recovery: Support post-event analysis, debriefs, and regulatory follow-up activities.

Cautions

  • Do not delay regulatory notification where required.
  • Ensure safety-related decisions are based on confirmed information.
  • Maintain clear records of safety advice and authority interactions.
  • Coordinate closely with OCC and operational leadership.
