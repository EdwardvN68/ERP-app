Commercial Director – Emergency Response Role

Activation

Activated during Full EMT mobilization, or when commercial implications of the emergency require immediate client/stakeholder engagement. The CD provides guidance on contract exposure, client notifications, and insurance-related decisions.

Refer to ERP Sections 2.5.2, 3.6.7, and Appendix C.10.

Core Responsibilities

  • Act as commercial liaison for client-facing matters during the emergency.
  • Support Accountable Manager in assessing contractual exposure and obligations.
  • Coordinate with ADA’s legal team and insurers regarding client correspondence.
  • Ensure commercial communication aligns with approved media releases.
  • Assist with cost tracking, client relationship stability, and future claims positioning.

ERP References

  • Section 2.5.2 – EMT Activation Criteria
  • Section 3.6.7 – Commercial Director
  • Appendix C.10 – CD Checklist
  • Appendix B.5 – Aircraft Accident Notification Form (if applicable)

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Notify commercial clients via pre-approved language. Join EMT briefings to receive situational updates. Begin reviewing contractual exposure and commitments.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Coordinate with insurers, legal team, and Safety Director to draft stakeholder updates. Assist communications team in preparing FAQ for external parties.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Track costs for commercial audit trail. Support claim submissions, contract renegotiation discussions, and reputation risk management.

Cautions

  • Do not issue commercial statements without EMT Chair and Communications Officer clearance.
  • Ensure all data shared with clients or insurers is verified by the Safety Director.
  • Coordinate legal liability discussions exclusively through ADA Legal and the AM.
  • All external emails must use ADA crisis communication templates.
