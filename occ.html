Operations Control Centre – Emergency Response Role

Activation

Refer to ERP Sections 2.5 (OCC activation of the EMT), 2.6 (OCC initial emergency response actions), and 3.6.12 (Operations Control Centre), and Appendices B.2 and B.7.

Trigger: Any reported emergency, abnormal event, or third-party notification involving ADA operations that requires OCC assessment and/or EMT activation consideration.

The Operations Control Centre (OCC) is active 24/7 and provides the initial coordination and communication response until the Emergency Management Team (EMT) is activated and assumes overall emergency management responsibilities.

Core Responsibilities

  • Operation of the Operations Control Centre is directly responsible to the Director of Operations.
  • Notify the Director of Operations of any incident and hand over the incident to the EMT when directed to do so by the Director of Operations.
  • Notify any incident to the Crisis Management Service Provider (Go Crisis).
  • Set aside specific staff from normal duties to provide information to assist the EMT with any call from aircrew or employees affected in the emergency.
  • Advise the EMT of a separate number for families to call.
  • Arrange for additional staffing if required.

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Assess the situation, contact the Duty Director of Operations (DOO) when EMT activation thresholds are met (or if in doubt), start logging actions, and apply the Initial Call to Operations checklist (Apdx B.2) where applicable.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Continue supporting the Director of Operations and EMT: maintain records (including the Incident Log Form – Apdx B.7), set aside staff to handle calls from affected aircrew/employees, and notify Go Crisis as required.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Maintain OCC support as directed: ensure continuity of staffing, provide the EMT with a separate family contact number as required, and arrange additional staffing if required.

Cautions

  • Contact the Duty Director of Operations (DOO) when Medium/High EMT activation thresholds are met, and if there is any doubt whether the threshold has been met.
  • Use the Incident Log Form (Apdx B.7) to log information received and actions taken, and maintain accurate time-stamped records.
  • If applying the Initial Call to Operations checklist (Apdx B.2), do not provide information to anyone other than Abu Dhabi Aviation Senior Managers.
  • Do not send any other information in the EMT emergency SMS beyond the message stated in Apdx B.2 (when instructed to activate call-out).
  • Ensure continuity of OCC staffing during extended events by arranging additional staffing if required.
