Operations Control Centre – Emergency Response Role

Activation

The Operations Control Centre (OCC) is active 24/7 and forms the first operational link in ADA’s emergency response chain. OCC personnel are activated for all emergency levels (Low, Medium, High), and are responsible for logging, notification, coordination, and communication tasks until command is transferred to the EMT.

Trigger: Any aviation-related emergency, abnormal event, or third-party notification involving ADA aircraft, staff, or operations.

Refer to ERP Sections 2.5.1, 2.6, and 3.6.10, and Appendix B.2 and B.6.

Core Responsibilities

  • Notify the Director of Operations of any incident and hand over responsibility to the EMT once instructed.
  • Maintain real-time documentation using Appendix B.6 – Incident Log Form.
  • Notify the Crisis Management Service Provider (Go Crisis) without delay.
  • Assign specific staff to support the EMT by handling emergency calls.
  • Provide the EMT with a dedicated family contact number.
  • Arrange for extra staffing as needed during extended operations.
  • Accurately match NOK details to each crew/passenger manifest.
  • Ensure no direct NOK contact until Police notification is confirmed.
  • Provide NOK contact details to Police only upon request.
  • Maintain a list of families/friends requesting updates and relay to EMT or FAST.
  • Instruct unaffected employees to contact their families to reduce concern.

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Verify incident, notify DOO, open Incident Log (B.6), start notification cascade, assign family number, alert Go Crisis.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Provide updates to DOO and EMT, maintain log entries, coordinate with OCC Manager, confirm crew/NOK info, relay updates to EMT/FAST.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Monitor operations, assist with EMT coordination, prepare log handover, archive records as per retention policy.

Cautions

  • Do not delay notification to the Director of Operations or DOO.
  • Never contact families directly unless Police notification is confirmed.
  • Use only authorized checklists and forms—Appendix B.2 and B.6.
  • Log all actions in real time.
  • Ensure continuity by assigning deputies during extended events.
  • Do not engage with the media — refer to Commercial Director or EMT spokesperson.
