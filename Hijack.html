SIMULATION SCENARIO: HIJACK

Training / awareness scenario (for learning context)
TRAINING SIMULATION – NOT FOR OPERATIONAL USE This page supports training and familiarisation only.

Scenario Summary

FlightADA412 – AUH to Al Khobar
AircraftDash 8 Q400 – A6-ADK
StatusIn flight (FL240)
Persons Onboard3 crew, 26 passengers
Show / Hide Simulation Timeline
  • 08:44 – TRANSPONDER ALERT (7500)
    • Air Traffic Control notifies the Operations Control Centre that the aircraft has selected transponder code 7500, indicating a potential unlawful interference situation • An ACARS message is received confirming a cockpit security concern, reinforcing the seriousness of the alert • The OCC Duty Coordinator immediately opens an Incident Log to formally capture the event and ensure traceability
    ERP 2.3 ERP 2.6.2 Form: Appendix B.6
  • 08:47 – OCC ESCALATION
    • The Duty Director of Operations is informed to ensure senior operational awareness of the developing security situation • The Accountable Manager is notified to provide executive oversight and support organisational escalation • The Quick Response Emergency Management Team is initiated to enable coordinated monitoring and response under the ERP
    ERP 2.5.1 ERP 3.3
  • 08:50 – EMT MOBILISED
    • The Emergency Management Centre is activated to provide a secure and controlled coordination environment • Core Emergency Management Team roles are mobilised to ensure operational, safety, security, communications, and liaison functions are covered • All communications are managed in a controlled manner to prevent misinformation and protect the integrity of the response
    ERP 3.3 ERP 3.5.1 ERP 3.6
  • 08:55 – AUTHORITY COORDINATION
    • Formal coordination is established with relevant national and aviation authorities to ensure aligned response oversight • Regulatory and emergency management entities are kept informed as the situation develops • Air Traffic Control continues active tracking of the aircraft and provides updates to support situational awareness
    ERP 2.3 ERP 3.6
  • 09:10 – MEDIA & INFORMATION CONTROL
    • A holding statement is drafted in anticipation of potential external enquiry, without releasing any unverified information • Controls are reinforced to ensure that no unauthorised internal or external communication takes place • Go-Crisis is notified and placed on standby should family or psychological support become necessary
    ERP 1.14 ERP 1.15
  • 09:22 – SITUATION DE-ESCALATED
    • The Pilot-in-Command reports that the security threat has been resolved • The aircraft transponder code is returned to normal, indicating a reduction in threat status • The flight continues toward its planned destination under monitored conditions
    ERP 3.5.1
  • 09:55 – SAFE LANDING
    • The aircraft lands safely at its destination without further incident • Local authorities secure the aircraft upon arrival as a precautionary measure • All crew and passengers are confirmed safe, with no injuries reported
    ERP 3.7
  • POST-EVENT – REVIEW & CLOSE-OUT
    • All incident documentation and records are uploaded to the SMS system to ensure traceability and audit readiness • An internal review is conducted to assess response effectiveness and coordination • Any corrective or improvement actions identified are logged for follow-up and governance oversight
    ERP 1.13 ERP 0.1
