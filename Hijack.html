SIMULATION SCENARIO: HIJACK

Title: Hijack In-Flight – ADA Dash 8 Q400 Charter to Saudi Arabia

Date: Thursday, 26 June 2025

Flight: ADA412 – Zayed International Airport (AUH) to Al Khobar, Saudi Arabia

Aircraft Type: Dash 8 Q400

Registration: A6-ADK

Crew: 2 pilots, 1 cabin crew

Passengers: 26 oilfield staff (ADNOC)

Status: Cruising FL240, 38 minutes after departure

08:44 – TRANSPONDER ALERT: HIJACK CODE 7500
  • ATC notifies OCC after receiving squawk 7500 from ADA412
  • ACARS message received: "ADA412. Cockpit security breached. We are under duress. Maintain silence."
  • OCC Duty Coordinator initiates Incident Log (Form B.6)
ERP References: ERP 2.9.1 – Hijack or Standby Hijack, ERP 2.3.1 – Report Details, Appendix B.6 – Incident Log
08:47 – FIRST ACTIONS BY OCC
  • OCC Duty Coordinator informs DOO
  • Escalates to Accountable Manager
  • Triggers immediate Quick Response EMT
ERP References: ERP 2.5.1 (HIGH classification), ERP 2.9.3 – OCC Response to Hijack
08:50 – EMT QUICK RESPONSE TEAM MOBILISED
  • EMC activated with:
    • DOO (EMT Chair)
    • Safety Director
    • EMC Manager
    • OCC Manager
    • Communications & Liaison Officer
    • Legal Advisor
ERP References: ERP 3.3.1, 3.5.1 | Checklists: Appendix C.1–C.6
08:55 – COORDINATION WITH AUTHORITIES
  • Liaison initiated with:
    • GCAA (Security Division)
    • NCEMA
    • KSA Civil Aviation Security
    • Abu Dhabi Police Aviation Division
    • ADNOC Emergency Desk (due to passenger profile)
ERP References: ERP 1.4.4, 1.15.6.2, ERP 2.9.3(c)
09:00 – TRACKING AND STATUS
  • ADA412 (A6-ADK) remains on original route
  • ATC maintains radar lock; ACARS heartbeat active
  • Flight path adjusted to isolated holding sector over desert
  • PIC confirms: no communication unless advised by GCAA
ERP References: ERP 2.9.1, 3.6.16
09:10 – MEDIA STRATEGY IN PLACE
  • Communications Officer drafts Holding Statement:
“ADA confirms it is managing an onboard security situation involving flight ADA412 (Dash 8 Q400 A6-ADK). The safety of all onboard is our priority. We are coordinating closely with national and international authorities.”
  • Staff reminded of non-disclosure under ERP 1.14
  • Go-Crisis notified for possible escalation
ERP References: ERP 1.15.1–1.15.4
09:22 – SITUATION CONTAINED
  • ACARS Transmission from PIC: “Threat de-escalated. No injuries. Aircraft stable. Request ATC clearance for destination.”
  • Transponder changed back to 2000
  • OCC advises continuation to Al Khobar under security escort
09:55 – SAFE LANDING IN AL KHOBAR
  • Aircraft lands safely at Executive Terminal
  • Saudi Aviation Police secure aircraft
  • Suspect identified: off-duty contractor attempting cockpit access under false credentials
  • No weapons, no injuries
10:20 – RECOVERY PHASE INITIATED
  • EMT Chair (DOO) initiates response stand-down
  • Safety Director begins investigative log preparation
  • Legal prepares report for GCAA
ERP References: ERP Stage Four – Recovery, ERP 3.7, 3.5.1
13:00 – FINAL DEBRIEF AND AUDIT TRIGGERS
  • Incident documents uploaded to SMS-Pro
  • OCC process reviewed against ERP 2.9.3
  • Key action: audit passenger vetting process and revise crew security protocols
ERP Reference: ERP 1.13
