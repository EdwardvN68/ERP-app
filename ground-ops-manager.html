Ground Operations Manager – Emergency Response Role

Activation

Activated during emergencies involving flight disruption, airport access, or apron operations. Also activated upon full EMT mobilisation or at the direction of the AM, DOO, or OCC. This role is essential for securing access, coordination, and readiness of ground support services.

Refer to ERP Sections 2.5.1, 3.6.8 and Appendix C.11.

Core Responsibilities

  • Provide detailed handling-related information to the Emergency Management Team (EMT) as requested.
  • Forward travel, accommodation, and logistics arrangements for next of kin, employees, and crew to relevant destination airports.
  • Identify skill and manpower needs for affected airports; release appropriately trained staff and inform the EMT of the resource and deployment plan.
  • Ensure cargo is preserved and secured until officially handed over to the designated investigation authority.
  • Obtain and document next of kin instructions regarding the handling of deceased persons and any personal effects recovered.
  • Quarantine and restrict access to escort and crew manifests.
  • Quarantine and secure cargo manifests; originals must be transported to the Emergency Management Centre (EMC) using secure methods.
  • Advise airport contractors on suitable actions to be taken in line with the emergency.
  • Notify the Family Support Team (FST) of any specific operational needs or sensitivities related to locations impacted by the emergency.

ERP References

  • Section 2.5.1 – EMT Activation Thresholds
  • Section 3.6.8 – Ground Operations Manager
  • Appendix C.11 – Ground Operations Manager Checklist

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Confirm availability, validate access to airport/apron, and initiate coordination with airport duty manager.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Secure ground services for any return flights, including staging, reception, and staging support for PR or FAM.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Support recovery logistics, document actions taken, and participate in post-event debriefs.

Cautions

  • Do not assume ground services are available — verify and confirm.
  • Do not engage airport media teams directly — route via PR.
  • Maintain accurate records of handovers, delays, and service gaps.
  • Ensure third-party personnel (Go Crisis, FAM) are escorted and cleared for apron-side access as needed.
