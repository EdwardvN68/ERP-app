Crisis Management Service Provider – Emergency Response Role

Activation

Activated during high-level emergencies or when external crisis response support is required. Go Crisis is formally activated by the Accountable Manager or Safety Director to provide communications, family assistance, and logistical services in accordance with their agreement.

Refer to ERP Sections 1.15.2, 2.5.2, 3.6.15 and Appendix A.1.

Core Responsibilities

  • Provide crisis communication support, including media coordination and drafting of public statements.
  • Support family assistance operations, including communications and welfare services for affected persons.
  • Deploy trained representatives to assist at ADA headquarters or at designated support locations.
  • Assist in post-event recovery, including psychological first aid and record-keeping support.
  • Operate strictly under EMT direction and maintain full compliance with GCAA and ADA procedures.

ERP References

  • Section 1.15.2 – Crisis Management Service Provider Services
  • Section 2.5.2 – EMT On-Call Roles
  • Section 3.6.15 – Crisis Management Service Provider (Go Crisis)
  • Appendix A.1 – Crisis Management Service Provider Agreement

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Confirm activation, establish contact with EMT, prepare response personnel and services.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Deploy to ADA premises, assist in communications, support family liaison, provide crisis logistics.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Maintain active support roles, facilitate recovery, support debriefing, submit reports.

Cautions

  • Go Crisis must only act upon formal activation from ADA management.
  • Must not issue any external communication without EMT coordination.
  • Actions must strictly follow the scope defined in the Crisis Response Agreement.
  • Support functions must be coordinated with EMT leads (e.g., Family Assistance, Media, Legal).
  • All support activity must be logged and documented for audit trail.
