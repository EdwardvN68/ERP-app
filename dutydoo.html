Duty Director of Operations (DOO) – Emergency Response Role

Activation

The DOO is activated in all emergency levels and serves as the initial coordination link between operations and the EMT.

Refer to ERP Sections 2.5, 2.6, 3.3 and Appendix C.4 – EMT Checklist.

Core Responsibilities

  • Act as senior ops authority during initial emergency stage
  • Coordinate initial response with OCC and Flight Ops
  • Classify the emergency (Minor, Medium, High)
  • Notify Accountable Manager and activate EMT if needed
  • Ensure Incident Log is opened and maintained
  • Coordinate with airport/regulator stakeholders
  • Prepare full handover if EMT activation occurs

ERP References

  • Section 2.5.2 – On-Call EMT Roles
  • Section 2.6 – Initial Emergency Communication
  • Section 3.3.3 – Emergency Management Organisation
  • Appendix C.4 – EMT Checklist
  • Form B.6 – Incident Log
  • Form B.5 – Aircraft Accident/Threat Notification

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Receive alert, verify aircraft status, coordinate OCC, determine level, open Form B.6, submit B.5 if needed, notify Safety Director
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Assist EMT transition, share ops details, monitor field activity, document all decisions
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Lead debrief, finalize logs, support investigation and recovery planning

Cautions

  • Do NOT delay emergency classification or escalation
  • Always use checklist and forms—no undocumented actions
  • Maintain situational control until formally relieved
  • Remain contactable by phone/radio throughout on-call duty
  • Keep Safety and OCC updated continuously
