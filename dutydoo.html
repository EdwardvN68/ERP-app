Duty Director of Operations (Emergency Management Team Member)

Role Context (Training Overview)

As Duty Director of Operations (DOO), you are the primary operational focal point during the early stages of an emergency. Your role bridges day-to-day flight operations, the Operations Control Centre (OCC), and the Emergency Management Team (EMT), ensuring timely escalation and continuity of operational control.

Activation (ERP-Aligned Awareness)

The DOO is engaged during all emergency levels. You play a key role in early classification, coordination, and escalation decisions until the EMT is fully established.

Refer to ERP Sections 2.5, 2.6, and 3.3.

Core Responsibilities (Explained for Training)

  • Provide senior operational oversight during the initial phase of an emergency.
  • Coordinate with OCC and flight operations to establish situational awareness.
  • Support emergency classification and escalation decisions.
  • Ensure appropriate notifications are made to senior management and EMT members.
  • Maintain continuity of operational control until formally relieved.
  • Support information flow to the EMT once activated.

Role Behaviour Across an Emergency (Training Interpretation)

  • Early phase: Confirm operational facts, coordinate OCC actions, and support escalation.
  • Sustained response: Maintain operational oversight and support EMT coordination.
  • Stabilisation: Support handover, debriefing, and recovery planning.

Cautions

  • Do not delay escalation when emergency thresholds are met.
  • Base decisions on verified operational information.
  • Maintain clear communication with OCC and EMT leadership.
  • Ensure continuity until formally relieved.
