Head of IT – Emergency Response Role

Activation

The Head of IT is activated during any Medium or High-level emergency requiring data protection, secure communications, or IT infrastructure support. This includes support for the EMT, remote access provisioning, data safeguarding, and forensic readiness.

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.12 – Head of IT.

Core Responsibilities

  • Ensure the continued functioning and security of all IT systems supporting the EMT and response operations.
  • Immediately quarantine data and devices related to the affected aircraft, crew, or operations. This includes computers, tablets, radios, mobile phones, and personal drives.
  • Assist in securing and isolating access to the flight crew’s email accounts, company login credentials, and system audit trails.
  • Coordinate with the Director of Quality and Legal Advisor for data retrieval, evidence preservation, and forensic imaging.
  • Restrict access to sensitive data systems to only essential personnel.
  • Provide the EMT with secure communication channels including encrypted email, access-controlled file sharing, and backup voice systems.
  • Restore or reroute critical IT services if existing systems become compromised.

ERP References

  • Section 3.6.12 – Head of IT

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Receive confirmation of activation from EMT Chair or Accountable Manager. Quarantine all relevant IT systems and personal devices associated with the flight. Disable non-essential access to sensitive systems and notify IT Security. Provide EMT access to secure communication platforms.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Initiate forensic backup of affected systems in coordination with Quality and Legal. Monitor all internal systems for attempted unauthorized access. Maintain communication system continuity for EMT use.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Provide IT support to Emergency Call Centre and Family Assistance Team if activated. Document all security actions taken for post-event audit. Begin restoration of services or workarounds as recovery phase begins.

Cautions

  • Do not permit any deletion or modification of potentially relevant data.
  • Do not delay system lockdowns—quarantine takes priority over convenience.
  • Avoid assumptions about system security—verify and log every action.
  • Do not use unsecured platforms for EMT communications during an active event.
