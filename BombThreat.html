SIMULATION SCENARIO: BOMB THREAT

Training / awareness scenario (for learning context)
TRAINING SIMULATION – NOT FOR OPERATIONAL USE This page supports training and familiarisation only.

Scenario Summary

Flight ADA208 – AUH to Al Ain
Aircraft Dash 8 Q400 – A6-ADM
Status On ground during boarding
Persons Onboard 3 crew, 18 passengers
Show / Hide Simulation Timeline
  • 06:25 – THREAT RECEIVED VIA PHONE
    • An anonymous telephone call is received by the Reception Operator reporting a bomb threat associated with an ADA flight • The content of the call is recorded verbatim to preserve accuracy and support subsequent assessment • The Operations Control Centre (OCC) is notified immediately to ensure escalation through the established emergency communication pathway
    ERP 2.3 ERP 2.6.2 Form: Appendix B.7 & B.8
  • 06:27 – OCC LOG OPENED
    • An Incident Log is opened by the OCC to formally record the threat and establish traceability from the first notification • The Duty Director of Operations (DOO) is informed to ensure senior operational awareness • The affected aircraft is confirmed as the only flight boarding for the stated destination, helping define the immediate scope of the event
    ERP 2.6.2 Form: Appendix B.6
  • 06:40 – EMT ACTIVATED (PRECAUTIONARY)
    • The Emergency Management Team (EMT) is activated on a precautionary basis due to the security-related nature of the threat • Relevant EMT roles are mobilised and convened within the Emergency Management Centre • The activation ensures coordinated oversight of safety, security, communications, and stakeholder engagement
    ERP 2.5.1 ERP 3.3 ERP 3.6
  • 06:42 – AIRCRAFT ISOLATED
    • The aircraft is repositioned to an isolated parking area to reduce exposure and manage risk • Passengers and crew are safely disembarked and accounted for under controlled conditions • Relevant authorities are present on site to conduct security assessment and clearance activities
    ERP 3.4.5 Checklist: Appendix C
  • 07:45 – SITUATION CLEARED
    • A full inspection is completed by the competent authorities with no device or threat item identified • The aircraft is formally cleared following completion of security checks • The flight is authorised to resume operations, subject to operational delay and recovery planning
    ERP 1.15 ERP 3.8.6
  • POST-EVENT – REVIEW & REPORTING
    • The incident is formally reported through the appropriate regulatory reporting channels • All records and supporting documentation are uploaded to the SMS system to ensure traceability and audit readiness • Review actions and observations are logged to support organisational learning and continuous improvement
    ERP 1.13 ERP 0.1
