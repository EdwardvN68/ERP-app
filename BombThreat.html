SIMULATION SCENARIO: BOMB THREAT

Title: Bomb Threat Received – Scheduled Flight to Al Ain

Date: Sunday, 29 June 2025

Flight: ADA208 – Zayed International Airport to Al Ain International Airport (AAN)

Aircraft Type: Dash 8 Q400

Registration: A6-ADM

Crew: 2 pilots, 1 cabin crew

Passengers: 18

Status: Aircraft on ground at AUH during boarding

06:25 – THREAT RECEIVED VIA PHONE
  • Reception Operator receives anonymous call:
    “There’s something on your plane to Al Ain. It’ll go off before it gets there.”
  • Caller hangs up without identifying aircraft or location
  • Reception follows ERP protocol and immediately informs OCC
ERP References: ERP 2.10.1, ERP 2.3, Appendix B.7
06:27 – OCC NOTIFIED AND LOG OPENED
  • OCC Duty Coordinator completes Form B.7 – Bomb Warning Report
  • Initiates Form B.6 – Incident Log
  • Notifies Duty Director of Operations (DOO)
  • Confirms aircraft ADA208 is only one boarding at AUH for Al Ain
ERP References: ERP 2.10.2, Appendix B.6, B.7
06:30 – THREAT ASSESSMENT TEAM (TAT) CONVENED
  • Members:
    • Safety Director
    • Security Manager
    • Ground Operations Manager
  • Actions Taken:
    • Evaluate credibility per ERP Bomb Threat Procedure
    • No specific demands made
    • No matching intelligence from national threat channels
ERP References: ERP 2.10.2, 3.6.2, 3.6.14
06:40 – EMT ACTIVATED (PRECAUTIONARY)
  • EMT Chair: Duty DOO
  • EMC Roles Mobilised:
    • Safety Director
    • Ground Ops Manager
    • OCC Manager
    • Communications Officer
    • Legal Advisor
ERP References: ERP 3.3.1, 3.6.16; Checklists: Appendix C.4, C.9, C.14
06:42 – AIRCRAFT ISOLATED
  • ADA208 moved to isolated parking position
  • All passengers and crew safely disembarked
  • Luggage removed and placed in secured area
  • Aircraft sealed pending search
  • Airport authorities and Civil Defense on site
ERP References: ERP 2.10.4, 2.13.5
06:50 – BOMB SQUAD ARRIVES
  • Abu Dhabi Police EOD unit begins full aircraft and baggage inspection
  • No device found after 40-minute sweep
  • Final clearance issued by police and Civil Aviation Authority
07:45 – SITUATION CLEARED
  • Aircraft returned to service
  • New crew assigned per fatigue policy
  • Passengers re-screened and re-boarded
  • Flight departs with 1 hr 20 min delay
ERP References: ERP 1.15.3, 3.8.6
08:15 – COMMUNICATIONS & FOLLOW-UP
  • Holding statement issued:
“ADA confirms that a scheduled flight to Al Ain was delayed following a phone-in threat that was later deemed non-credible by security authorities. All passengers and crew were safe at all times. The aircraft departed after full security clearance.”
  • No passenger names disclosed
  • Incident report submitted to GCAA
  • Event uploaded to SMS-Pro
ERP References: ERP 1.15.5, 1.15.4, 1.13
