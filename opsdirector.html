Operations Director (Emergency Management Team Member)

Role Context (Training Overview)

As Operations Director, your role within the Emergency Management Team (EMT) is to provide senior oversight of flight operations impacts during emergencies. You ensure the EMT has clear operational information, understands the operational consequences of decisions, and can coordinate safely and effectively with OCC and other operational stakeholders.

Activation (ERP-Aligned Awareness)

The Operations Director is engaged during Medium and High-level emergencies and forms part of the EMT. Your focus is to oversee flight operations coordination and manage operational disruption at a strategic level.

Training note: This page supports role understanding and training. It does not replace ERP checklists or operational procedures used during an actual event.

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.3 and Appendix C.7 – Operations Director Checklist.

Core Responsibilities (Explained for Training)

Your responsibilities focus on ensuring that operational information, records, and flight operations impacts are available to support EMT decision-making. These responsibilities are strategic and coordination-based, not tactical “how-to” tasks.

  • Advise the EMT on all flight operations issues and operational impacts.
  • Ensure aircraft and operational data is available to support situational awareness.
  • Ensure relevant operational records are secured and available for appropriate stakeholders when required.
  • Support operational continuity planning, including relief crew considerations where applicable.
  • Support compilation and availability of flight documentation that may be required by the EMT and relevant authorities, such as:
    • Passenger manifest
    • Fuel planning and receipts
    • Weight and balance plans
    • Pre-flight planning documents
    • Weather reports
    • Flight and duty time records
    • Copy of crew training files
    • Logbook extracts (as applicable)
    • Medical certificates
    • Licences

Role Behaviour Across an Emergency (Training Interpretation)

Training note: This section provides role understanding for learning purposes. It does not replace ERP checklists and does not provide step-by-step procedures.

  • Early phase: Establish operational facts, coordinate with OCC, and support initial EMT situational awareness.
  • Sustained response: Provide operational impact updates, support safe coordination decisions, and ensure continuity planning is considered.
  • Stabilisation / recovery: Support recovery planning, post-event coordination, and operational debrief inputs as required.

Cautions

  • Ensure operational decisions are based on verified information and coordinated through OCC.
  • Maintain clear records of operational decisions and communications.
  • Consider operational risk and disruption impacts before supporting significant operational changes.
  • Ensure information provided to the EMT is accurate, timely, and traceable.
