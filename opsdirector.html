Operations Director – Emergency Response Role

Activation

Activated during Medium and High-level emergencies and forms part of the Emergency Management Team (EMT). Main function: oversee flight operations coordination and manage operational disruptions.

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.3 and Appendix C.7 – Operations Director Checklist.

Core Responsibilities

  • Advise EMT on all flight operations issues
  • Ensure aircraft operational data is available
  • Ensure flight crew records are secured by the Director of Quality
  • Arrange aerial charts/photos of affected airport
  • Coordinate relief flight crews as needed
  • Compile and provide flight documentation to EMT and Authorities:
    • Passenger manifest
    • Fuel planning and receipts
    • Weight and balance plans
    • Pre-flight planning documents
    • Weather reports
    • Flight and duty time records
    • Copy of crew training files
    • Logbook
    • Medical certificates
    • Licenses

ERP References

  • Section 3.6.3 – Operations Director Emergency Responsibilities
  • Appendix C.7 – Operations Director Checklist
  • Form B.6 – Incident Log
  • ERP 2.6 – Communication Channels

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Coordinate with OCC, suspend flights if needed, notify ground ops, update DOO and AM, account for crew
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Liaise with airport, approve alternate airports, supervise stranded passenger recovery
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Manage long-term recovery, submit ops impact report, support post-event debriefs

Cautions

  • Do not approve flights without full risk analysis
  • Verify aircraft status with OCC and Maintenance
  • Ensure GCAA is informed of route/ops changes
  • Document all operational directives in the log
