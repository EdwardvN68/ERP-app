Quality Director – Emergency Management Role

Activation Criteria

This role is activated as part of the Full Emergency Management Team (EMT) in accordance with ERP Section 2.5.2. The Quality Director ensures regulatory compliance and audit readiness throughout the emergency response cycle.

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.6 (Quality Director) and Appendix C.10.

Core Responsibilities

ERP Section 3.6.6 outlines the following duties:

  • Advising the EMT on all quality and compliance issues.
  • Ensuring that all Company documentation and records are properly controlled and available (e.g. manuals, certifications and other company/employee records).
  • Liaising the authorities, e.g. GCAA, other authorities (as required).
  • Participating, as required, in respect to post accident investigations.
  • Ensuring compliance with any applicable regulatory requirements.

Checklist Reference

Appendix C.10 – Quality Director Checklist must be followed, including:

  • Acknowledge callout and proceed to EMC, do not call for information.
  • Provide immediate advice on quality and compliance implications and issues as necessary; determine any specialist that may be required.
  • Ensure that all regulatory documentations and records are controlled and available.
  • Assist the EMT as required in respect to investigation and follow-up activities.
  • In the event of emergency, quality staff are to be instructed by the EMT and should ensure that all quality documentation and records are controlled and secured.
  • Ensure compliance with any regulatory requirements.
  • Ensure all matters are controlled and procedures are being followed and where necessary communicate to the EMC Chairman on any deviation and recommend corrective actions.
  • Ensure all reports are recorded and filed under the control of the EMC chairman. Ensure drafts are clearly marked “Draft”.

Response Phases

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Monitor EMT activation, verify checklist compliance, support OCC and Safety Director in process control.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Begin parallel audit of ERP implementation, track document control integrity, ensure traceability of logs.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Prepare GCAA-compliant audit trail, assist in evidence preservation protocols, coordinate internal quality statement to CEO and AM.

Restrictions & Compliance

  • All QA reports must be confidential and routed through the EMT chain of command.
  • Quality assurance inputs must not override emergency actions unless safety or legality is compromised.
  • Ensure all ERP forms, logs, and checklists are archived to SMS-Pro with traceability for audit.
