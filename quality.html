Quality Director – Emergency Management Role

Activation Criteria

This role is activated as part of the Full Emergency Management Team (EMT) in accordance with ERP Section 2.5.2. The Quality Director ensures regulatory compliance and audit readiness throughout the emergency response cycle.

Core Responsibilities

ERP Section 3.6.5 outlines the following duties:

  • Act as the primary authority on compliance and quality assurance during the incident response.
  • Ensure ERP checklists, forms, and timelines are followed in accordance with GCAA and internal audit standards.
  • Initiate internal QA reporting structures to track deviations, delays, or process failures in real time.
  • Support the Accountable Manager and Safety Director in documenting actions taken for post-event audit.
  • Facilitate post-response internal reviews and track effectiveness of corrective actions.

Checklist Reference

Appendix C.9 – Quality Director Checklist must be followed, including:

  • Confirm EMT procedural adherence
  • Document all timeline deviations or policy gaps
  • Initiate QA review of ERP implementation against SMS procedures

Response Phases

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Monitor EMT activation, verify checklist compliance, support OCC and Safety Director in process control.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Begin parallel audit of ERP implementation, track document control integrity, ensure traceability of logs.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Prepare GCAA-compliant audit trail, assist in evidence preservation protocols, coordinate internal quality statement to CEO and AM.

Restrictions & Compliance

  • All QA reports must be confidential and routed through the EMT chain of command.
  • Quality assurance inputs must not override emergency actions unless safety or legality is compromised.
  • Ensure all ERP forms, logs, and checklists are archived to SMS-Pro with traceability for audit.
