Accountable Manager – Emergency Response Role

Activation

Activated during High-level emergencies or when the Emergency Management Team (EMT) is fully mobilized. The AM acts as Chairman of the EMT and holds overall authority for directing the emergency response.

Refer to ERP Sections 2.5.1, 2.5.2, 3.3.2, 3.6.1 and Appendix C.5.

Core Responsibilities

  • Overall authority for directing the emergency response and leading the EMT.
  • Ensure continuity of company operations not impacted by the emergency.
  • Initiate and chair conference calls with airport and authority representatives.
  • Determine need to activate the Emergency Management Centre (EMC).
  • Authorize activation of the Crisis Management Service Provider (GoCrisis).
  • Monitor developments and provide regular updates to top management.

ERP References

  • Section 2.5.1 – EMT Activation Thresholds
  • Section 2.5.2 – EMT On-Call Roles
  • Section 3.3.2 – Full EMT Organisation Chart
  • Section 3.6.1 – Accountable Manager (Emergency Management Chairman)

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Verify incident, determine response level, authorize EMT activation, launch GoCrisis, brief senior management.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Chair EMT meetings, oversee info collection, coordinate with internal and external parties, ensure situational awareness.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Direct transition to recovery, lead debriefings, approve final reports and archiving.

Cautions

  • Do not delay EMT activation in high-severity emergencies.
  • Base decisions only on confirmed information — avoid assumptions.
  • All records must be securely retained.
  • Ensure EMT members are not overworked — manage fatigue risk.
  • Delegate administration, but maintain strategic oversight throughout.
