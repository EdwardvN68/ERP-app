Accountable Manager (Emergency Management Chairman)

Activation

In the ERP structure, the Accountable Manager (AM) provides the highest level of leadership within the Emergency Management Team (EMT). During high-level emergencies that require full EMT mobilisation, the AM acts as Chairman of the EMT, providing strategic direction and overall authority for the organisational response.

Training note: Activation thresholds and mobilisation levels are defined in the ERP. This training page explains how the AM role functions within that framework; it does not replace the ERP checklists used during an actual event.

Refer to ERP Sections 2.5.1, 2.5.2, 3.3.2, 3.6.1 and Appendix C.5.

Core Responsibilities

As AM (EMT Chairman), your responsibilities are primarily strategic leadership and assurance—you set direction, authorise key actions, and ensure the EMT functions effectively. Your role is not to perform tactical tasks; it is to ensure the organisation responds decisively and correctly.

  • Provide overall direction for emergency response and lead the EMT as Chairman.
  • Ensure continuity of company activities that are not affected by the incident, where safe to do so.
  • Convene and chair high-level conference calls, including with relevant airports and authorities when required.
  • Determine whether activation of the Emergency Management Centre (EMC) is necessary.
  • Act as the authorising authority for activation of the Crisis Management Service Provider (Go Crisis).
  • Monitor developments and provide regular updates to top management as often as required.

Role Behaviour Across an Emergency (Training Interpretation)

Training note: This section provides role understanding and expectations for learning purposes. It does not replace ERP checklists and does not provide step-by-step procedures.

  • Early phase: Establish leadership presence, confirm reliable information, ensure the correct EMT structure is in place, and set strategic priorities.
  • Sustained response: Chair EMT discussions, ensure coordination across internal functions and external stakeholders, and maintain organisational focus and discipline.
  • Stabilisation / recovery: Direct transition toward recovery and normalisation, ensure lessons are captured, and confirm appropriate closure and documentation.

Cautions

The Accountable Manager’s effectiveness depends on judgement, clarity, and control under pressure. Keep the following in mind:

  • Do not delay EMT mobilisation when severity criteria are met.
  • Base decisions only on confirmed information — avoid assumptions.
  • All records must be securely retained.
  • Ensure EMT members are not overworked — manage fatigue risk.
  • Delegate administration, but maintain strategic oversight throughout.
