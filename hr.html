Human Resources – Emergency Response Role

Activation

Activated during Quick Response or Full EMT activation, particularly where ADA personnel, contractors, or families are affected. The HR Manager manages all welfare, notification, and support processes.

Refer to ERP Sections 2.5.2, 3.6.6, and Appendix C.15.

Core Responsibilities

  • Serve as welfare lead for affected ADA staff, contractors, and passengers.
  • Coordinate with Family Assistance Coordinator (FAC) to manage NOK notification.
  • Ensure psychological and logistical support for staff and their families.
  • Coordinate shift rotations for responders to manage fatigue and mental wellbeing.
  • Maintain updated emergency contact details and verify crew/passenger manifest status.
  • Liaise with GoCrisis for joint staff and family support services.

ERP References

  • Section 2.5.2 – EMT On-Call Roles
  • Section 3.6.6 – HR Manager Role
  • Appendix C.15 – HR Checklist
  • Appendix D – Family Assistance Team Guidelines

Phase-Based Duties

  • Phase 1 (0–60 min): Deploy to EMC, confirm employee status, begin NOK validation, liaise with EMT Chair.
  • Phase 2 (1–4 hrs): Coordinate NOK notification, staff reassignment, support team fatigue management, activate FAC.
  • Phase 3 (4+ hrs): Oversee long-term welfare tracking, assist post-event debriefs, update HR records, support incident recovery audit.

Cautions

  • Do not release NOK information unless confirmed by EMT Chair or Safety Director.
  • Coordinate all public-facing communication with the Communications Officer.
  • Be sensitive to cultural protocols and emotional states when notifying families.
  • Track all personnel reassignment and welfare logs for audit purposes.
  • Document all staff interactions via Incident Log (Appendix B.6).
