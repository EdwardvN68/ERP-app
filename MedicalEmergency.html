SIMULATION SCENARIO: MEDICAL EMERGENCY

Training / awareness scenario (for learning context)
TRAINING SIMULATION – NOT FOR OPERATIONAL USE This page supports training and familiarisation only.

Scenario Summary

Flight ADA318 – AUH to Sir Bani Yas
Aircraft Dash 8 Q400 – A6-ADM
Status In flight
Persons Onboard 3 crew, 19 passengers
Show / Hide Simulation Timeline
  • 07:15 – PASSENGER MEDICAL EMERGENCY REPORTED
    • Approximately 28 minutes into the flight, cabin crew report an unresponsive passenger seated in 3C with shallow breathing • Initial information indicates the passenger is a 72-year-old male with a known cardiac history, as confirmed by a fellow passenger • Cabin crew commence onboard emergency response using CPR and the aircraft’s Automated External Defibrillator (AED) • The Captain is alerted and declares PAN-PAN to Air Traffic Control to prioritise handling of the medical emergency
    ERP 2.14 ERP 2.3
  • 07:18 – OCC NOTIFIED
    • The OCC Duty Coordinator is informed of the in-flight medical emergency and formally logs the event • The Duty Director of Operations is notified to ensure senior operational awareness • The event is categorised as a medical emergency for coordination and reporting purposes • Air Traffic Control confirms that the aircraft is returning to Zayed International Airport
    ERP 2.6.2 Form: Appendix B.6
  • 07:25 – DIVERSION DECISION CONFIRMED
    • The Pilot-in-Command confirms the decision to divert the flight back to AUH in response to the medical emergency • Airport medical services are alerted to prepare for immediate assistance on arrival • Go-Crisis is placed on watch as a precautionary measure should family notification or additional support be required
    ERP 2.14 ERP 2.5.1
  • 07:45 – SAFE LANDING AND MEDICAL HANDOVER
    • The Dash 8 aircraft lands safely at Zayed International Airport without further incident • Paramedics board the aircraft immediately to take over medical care • The passenger is stabilised with oxygen support and AED assistance before evacuation to medical services • The Captain completes the required in-flight medical reporting in accordance with ERP requirements
    ERP 3.7
  • POST-EVENT – REPORTING AND REVIEW
    • Incident documentation is completed to capture the details of the medical event and response • The event is recorded in the SMS system to ensure traceability and audit readiness • Follow-up actions and observations are reviewed to support learning and continuous improvement
    ERP 1.13 ERP 0.1
