SIMULATION SCENARIO: AIRCRAFT MISSING

Title: ADA AW139 Missing Over Desert Corridor

Date/Time: Wednesday, 25 June 2025

Flight: ADA212 – AUH to ADNOC Desert Wellhead 17

Aircraft Type: AW139

Registration: A6-AWP

Onboard: 2 pilots, 4 ADNOC technicians

Weather: Daylight VMC, 39°C, winds 14 knots NW

03:15 – FLIGHT DEPARTS AUH
  • Filed with OCC: AUH – Wellhead 17
  • Callsign: A6-AWP
  • Position Reports due every 15 minutes
  • OCC confirms flight departed on time; last Ops Normal received at 03:30
ERP Reference: 2.6.4
03:45 – POSITION REPORT NOT RECEIVED
  • No Ops Normal or position report received
  • First contact attempt via VHF (122.8 MHz), HF and SATPHONE — no response
  • OCC Duty Coordinator begins Incident Log (Form B.6)
ERP References: ERP 2.6.6 “Aircraft Overdue”, 2.3.1 | Checklist: Appendix B.3 | Forms: B.6, B.5
03:50 – DECLARED AIRCRAFT OVERDUE
  • As per ERP 2.6.6(a)(1): overdue if no Ops Normal within 5 minutes of ETA
  • ETA for next position: 03:45 → no contact by 03:50 = officially overdue
  • Initial actions:
    • Contacted point of departure and destination (ERP 2.6.6(b)(1))
    • Confirmed aircraft did not return or divert
04:00 – EMT ACTIVATION INITIATED
  • Declared HIGH emergency (ERP 2.5.1)
  • DOO activates Quick Response EMT
  • Authorizes Search & Rescue Mobilization (ERP 2.6.7)
ERP References: 2.5.1, 2.6.7, 2.6.9
04:20 – EMT MEETING IN EMC
  • Chair: DOO
  • Roles mobilized:
    • Safety Director
    • OCC Manager
    • HR Manager
    • Communications & Liaison Officer
    • EMC Manager
ERP Sections: 3.3.1, 3.5.1, 3.6
04:30 – SEARCH ACTION COMMENCED
  • OCC Manager directs:
    • Nearest ADA aircraft to intercept flight path
    • Coordination with ADNOC and Police Air Wing
    • Ground units and desert convoy mobilized
    • ADNOC platforms contacted for radio relay
ERP Reference: 2.6.7 | Checklist: Appendix C.25
04:45 – MEDIA & FAMILY PREPARATION (PROACTIVE STANCE)
  • FAC on standby, no media alert yet
  • HR drafts potential notifications
  • Go-Crisis informed, not activated
  • Communications Officer drafts holding statement, holds release
ERP References: 1.14, 1.15.1, 4.2
05:00 – OPERATIONS SHIFT CONTINUES FLIGHT COVERAGE
  • OCC continues operations in parallel with search
  • Search teams update position every 15 minutes
  • ADNOC confirms no distress calls via offshore repeaters
05:20 – AIRCRAFT LOCATED
  • Visual confirmation: landed on dry plateau 6NM off track
  • Crew alive, minor injuries
  • ELT failed to auto-activate
  • Electrical system failure, precautionary landing
  • Evacuation in progress
05:30 – RESPONSE LEVEL DEESCALATED
  • EMT chair authorizes stand-down
  • Full debrief scheduled for 10:00
  • Communications Officer issues statement:
“The missing helicopter has been located safely. All occupants are accounted for and receiving assistance. Full details to follow.”
  • FAC stood down
  • EMT prepares closeout
10:00 – INCIDENT DEBRIEF & SMS REVIEW
  • DOO chairs full EMT debrief
  • Incident Log B.6 uploaded to SMS-Pro
  • ELT malfunction submitted for engineering review
  • Lessons captured for readiness audit
  • ERP Review initiated per ERP 1.13 and 0.1
  • GCAA notified, no formal report required

Follow-Up Actions:

  • Maintenance inspection of AW139
  • OCC team commended for correct threshold handling
  • FST commended for efficient tracking coordination
  • Emergency drill planned to simulate ELT failure scenarios
