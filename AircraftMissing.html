SIMULATION SCENARIO: AIRCRAFT MISSING

Training / awareness scenario (for learning context)
TRAINING SIMULATION – NOT FOR OPERATIONAL USE This page presents an exercise timeline and ERP references to support learning and awareness. It does not provide operational instructions or decision-making guidance.

Scenario Summary

Scenario Title ADA AW139 Missing Over Desert Corridor
Date/Time Wednesday, 25 June 2025
Flight ADA212 — AUH to ADNOC Desert Wellhead 17
Aircraft / Reg AW139 — A6-AWP
Onboard 2 pilots, 4 ADNOC technicians
Weather Daylight VMC, 39°C, winds 14 kt NW
Show / Hide Simulation Timeline
  • 03:15 – FLIGHT DEPARTS AUH
    • The flight departs Zayed International Airport under normal operating conditions and is monitored by the OCC • The planned routing from AUH to Desert Wellhead 17 is filed and acknowledged for tracking purposes • The aircraft operates under the assigned callsign, with routine position reporting expected at 15-minute intervals • The OCC confirms the flight departed on time, with the last normal operational update received at 03:30
    References:
    ERP 2.6.2
  • 03:45 – POSITION REPORT NOT RECEIVED
    • A scheduled position report or Ops Normal update is not received within the expected reporting window • Initial contact attempts are made using VHF, HF, and SATPHONE communication methods, with no response from the aircraft • The OCC Duty Coordinator recognises the deviation from normal reporting and initiates formal incident logging • The Incident Log is opened using the appropriate form to ensure traceability from the first confirmed anomaly
    References:
    ERP 2.6.2 ERP 2.3.1 Checklist: Appendix B.3 Form: Appendix B.7
  • 03:50 – DECLARED AIRCRAFT OVERDUE
    • The aircraft is treated as overdue after the expected reporting time passes without successful contact • The OCC confirms that the aircraft has not returned to its departure point or diverted to an alternate location • Points of departure and destination are contacted to verify that no unreported landing has occurred • The situation is escalated internally to reflect increased concern and the potential need for coordinated response
    References:
    ERP 2.6.2
  • 04:00 – EMT ACTIVATION INITIATED
    • The situation is classified as a high-level emergency due to continued loss of contact with the aircraft • The Duty Director of Operations activates the Quick Response Emergency Management Team • Initial search coordination activity begins to support aircraft location and situational awareness
    References:
    ERP 2.5.1 ERP 3.3.1
  • 04:20 – EMT MEETING IN EMC
    • The Emergency Management Team convenes in the Emergency Management Centre under the chairmanship of the DOO • Core functional roles assemble to provide operational control, safety oversight, people support, and coordination • The meeting establishes shared situational awareness and confirms early response priorities in line with the ERP
    References:
    ERP 3.3.1 ERP 3.5.1 ERP 3.6
  • 04:30 – SEARCH ACTION COMMENCED
    • Search activity is initiated under OCC coordination to locate the missing aircraft • The nearest available ADA aircraft is tasked to intercept the planned flight path • Coordination is established with ADNOC and Police Air Wing assets to extend aerial search coverage • Ground units and desert convoys are mobilised to support surface search and access • ADNOC sites along the route are contacted to provide radio relay support where possible
    References:
    ERP 3.4.5 Checklist: Appendix C.25
  • 04:45 – MEDIA & FAMILY PREPARATION (PROACTIVE STANCE)
    • Family Assistance arrangements are placed on standby as a precautionary measure • The HR function prepares potential next-of-kin notifications should escalation be required • Go-Crisis is informed of the situation but not activated at this stage • A draft holding statement is prepared by the Communications Officer but intentionally not released
    References:
    ERP 1.14 ERP 1.15 ERP Chapter 5
  • 05:00 – OPERATIONS SHIFT CONTINUES FLIGHT COVERAGE
    • OCC maintains normal flight operations in parallel with the ongoing search activity • Search teams provide regular updates to the OCC at agreed reporting intervals • ADNOC confirms that no distress calls have been received through available repeater systems
    References:
    ERP 2.6.2
  • 05:20 – AIRCRAFT LOCATED
    • The aircraft is visually located on a dry plateau approximately six nautical miles off the planned route • All occupants are confirmed alive, with only minor injuries reported • It is determined that the ELT did not automatically activate following the event • A precautionary landing was carried out due to an electrical system failure • Evacuation and recovery arrangements are initiated to support crew, passengers, and aircraft
    References:
    ERP 3.4.5
  • 05:30 – RESPONSE LEVEL DE-ESCALATED
    • The EMT Chair authorises a controlled stand-down following confirmation that the aircraft and occupants are safe • A full incident debrief is scheduled to review the response and capture lessons • An external communication is issued confirming safe location of the aircraft and wellbeing of occupants • Family Assistance arrangements are formally stood down • The EMT begins close-out activities in preparation for recovery and review
    References:
    ERP 3.5.1 ERP 1.15
  • 10:00 – INCIDENT DEBRIEF & SMS REVIEW
    • The Duty Director of Operations chairs a full Emergency Management Team debrief • Incident logs and supporting records are uploaded to the SMS system to ensure traceability • The ELT malfunction is formally submitted for engineering investigation and review • Lessons identified are captured to support readiness assurance and future training • An ERP review is initiated to confirm continued effectiveness • Follow-up actions are identified, including maintenance inspection, internal recognition, and future emergency drill planning
    References:
    ERP 1.13 ERP 0.1
