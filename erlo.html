Emergency Response Liaison Officer – Emergency Response Role

Activation

The Emergency Response Liaison Officer (ERLO) is activated during Medium and High-level emergencies requiring coordination with external authorities, agencies, and stakeholders. The ERLO serves as ADA’s official liaison with national bodies, airport entities, civil aviation authorities, and other involved government organizations.

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.18 (subsections 3.6.18.1 to 3.6.18.10) for complete role responsibilities.

Core Responsibilities

3.6.18.1 National Coordination and Preparedness

  • Coordinate with the GCAA Emergency Operations Room and relevant authorities at the Abu Dhabi Joint Centre.
  • Participate in developing national/local emergency plans with specialised authorities.
  • Engage in joint exercises, training, and emergency-related community events.
  • Conduct national resource inventorying and evaluation; define utilisation priorities.
  • Maintain coordination with key representatives of related authorities.
  • Propose enhancements to the emergency/crisis/disaster management system.
  • Coordinate with internal departments involved in emergency response.
  • Propose specialised emergency scenarios to improve readiness.
  • Draft and maintain relevant Memorandums of Understanding (MoUs).

3.6.18.2 Organisational Familiarity & Representation

  • Understand ADA's duties as a supporting or leading entity under approved plans.
  • Be familiar with operations centre contacts and data exchange mechanisms.
  • Accurately convey ADA’s operational status, actions taken, and decision rationale.
  • Obtain the latest situational updates and report to ADA’s operations centre.
  • Serve in the operations centre team if requested.
  • Act as ADA’s primary liaison with GCAA and NCEMA.

3.6.18.3 Family Assistance and Humanitarian Support

  • Coordinate with family assistance teams to ensure clear, timely, and compassionate communication.
  • Facilitate logistics for affected families (transportation, accommodation, emotional support).

3.6.18.4 Field Response and Site Coordination

  • Coordinate with ADA’s Emergency Response Teams.
  • Support crash site responders (SAR, medical, recovery teams) to ensure efficient collaboration.
  • Communicate crash site logistical needs to relevant agencies for timely support.

3.6.18.5 Information Gathering and Real-Time Reporting

  • Collect reliable data on incident status, passenger impact, and response progress.
  • Provide frequent updates to ADA’s crisis management and executive leadership.

3.6.18.6 Media & Public Information Interface

Coordinate with ADA’s internal media team to ensure accurate, official public messaging.

3.6.18.7 Regulatory & Investigative Support

Liaise with GCAA and NCEMA during investigations; ensure crash site access and documentation.

3.6.18.8 Documentation & Incident Monitoring

  • Maintain a log of all emergency actions and decisions for audits/legal reviews.
  • Track progress of incident response; notify leadership of any risks or delays.

3.6.18.9 Recovery and Post-Incident Evaluation

  • Coordinate recovery and aftercare efforts with involved agencies.
  • Participate in post-incident evaluations to strengthen ADA’s crisis response.
  • Document lessons learned; propose updates to training, resources, or protocols.

3.6.18.10 Leadership Role

Act as the FST Team Leader during emergency response operations.

Cautions

  • Do not issue ADA statements to the media or agencies unless directed by the EMT.
  • Do not share internal information unless specifically approved by the EMT or Accountable Manager.
  • Ensure alignment with government protocols—never act independently of established national procedures.
  • Keep detailed logs of all engagements, meetings, and agency discussions.
