Emergency Response Liaison Officer – Emergency Response Role

Activation

The Emergency Response Liaison Officer (ERLO) is activated during Medium and High-level emergencies requiring coordination with external authorities, agencies, and stakeholders. The ERLO serves as ADA’s official liaison with national bodies, airport entities, civil aviation authorities, and other involved government organizations.

Refer to ERP Section 3.6.16 (subsections .1 to .10) for complete role responsibilities.

Core Responsibilities

  • 3.6.16.1 – National Coordination and Preparedness: Align ADA’s ERP with national emergency plans. Maintain working relationships with NCEMA, GCAA, and airport authorities.
  • 3.6.16.2 – Organisational Familiarity and Representation: Represent ADA during exercises and emergency coordination meetings. Understand ADA’s emergency structure and plans.
  • 3.6.16.3 – Family Assistance and Humanitarian Support: Act as liaison to humanitarian agencies. Support coordination with Red Crescent, Go Crisis, etc.
  • 3.6.16.4 – Field Response and Site Coordination: Provide on-site liaison at the incident site. Interface with Incident Command System when activated.
  • 3.6.16.5 – Information Gathering and Reporting: Collect and share updates from national agencies to ADA EMT. Monitor official communication channels.
  • 3.6.16.6 – Media and Public Information Interface: Ensure alignment of ADA media messaging with government authorities. Monitor official press releases and report to EMT.
  • 3.6.16.7 – Regulatory and Investigative Support: Assist investigators and authorities with access and documentation. Coordinate only with EMT approval.
  • 3.6.16.8 – Documentation and Monitoring: Log all external engagements. Maintain a record for post-event evaluation.
  • 3.6.16.9 – Recovery and Post-Incident Evaluation: Participate in recovery and support inter-agency coordination. Attend debriefs and report outcomes to EMT.
  • 3.6.16.10 – Leadership Role: Serve as the senior liaison with national bodies if delegated. Advise EMT on inter-agency expectations and obligations.

ERP References

Cautions

  • Do not issue ADA statements to the media or agencies unless directed by the EMT.
  • Do not share internal information unless specifically approved by the EMT or Accountable Manager.
  • Ensure alignment with government protocols—never act independently of established national procedures.
  • Keep detailed logs of all engagements, meetings, and agency discussions.
